🎮 GAME 1: Beat the Budget

Concept: Opportunity Cost + Allocation

Prompt for Replit AI Agent

Build a compact interactive budgeting game called “Beat the Budget.”

Target age: 10–16
Time to complete: 5–7 minutes

Core mechanic:

User starts with $100 weekly allowance.

Show 6 spending options (3 wants, 2 needs, 1 surprise expense).

User must allocate funds by selecting items.

Include at least 1 unexpected expense after 2–3 selections.

Technical Requirements:

Use a simple card selection UI.

Deduct funds in real time.

Display remaining balance dynamically.

Add a “Future Self Score” at the end (0–100).

Scoring Logic:

Reward savings

Reward covering needs first

Penalize running out of money

Penalize not preparing for surprise expense

End screen must show:

What they did well

What they could improve

A short 2–3 sentence educational takeaway

Keep UI colorful but clean. No advanced analytics. Keep logic simple and easy to modify.

📈 GAME 2: Compound It

Concept: Compound Interest Visualization

Prompt for Replit AI Agent

Build a visual compound interest simulator called “Compound It.”

Target age: 10–16
Completion time: 3–5 minutes

Core interaction:

Slider for weekly savings ($5–$50)

Slider for years (1–20)

Fixed 7% simulated annual return

Requirements:

Show animated growth chart

Show total contributed vs total earned

Make growth visually accelerate over time

Add satisfying visual feedback when time slider increases

At the bottom:

Display a simple explanation of why growth speeds up

Show “You earned $X without working more”

No advanced finance math explanation.
Focus on visual emotional impact.

⚡ GAME 3: Needs vs Wants Speed Round

Concept: Spending Awareness

Prompt for Replit AI Agent

Build a rapid categorization game called “Needs vs Wants Speed Round.”

Target age: 8–14
Completion time: 2–4 minutes

Gameplay:

Show 20 items one at a time.

User must click “Need” or “Want” within 3 seconds.

Show immediate correct/incorrect feedback.

Add:

Countdown timer

Score counter

Final accuracy percentage

After completion:

Show 2–3 reflection questions:

“Why is this a need?”

“Would this change if you were older?”

Keep visuals playful.
No heavy explanations.
Make it feel energetic.

💰 GAME 4: Future Me

Concept: Delayed Gratification

Prompt for Replit AI Agent

Build a decision game called “Future Me.”

Target age: 10–16
Completion time: 3–5 minutes

Core mechanic:

Present 5 rounds of choices:

Example: $50 today vs $200 in 1 year

$10 now vs $30 next month

User chooses one per round.

After all rounds:

Show total short-term gains

Show total long-term gains

Show comparison difference

Add:

Visual timeline for delayed rewards

Simple explanation of patience and growth

Do not shame short-term decisions.
Instead, explain trade-offs clearly.

Keep UI clean and intuitive.
